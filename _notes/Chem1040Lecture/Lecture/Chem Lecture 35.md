# Ideal Gas Law
PV = nRT or V = $\frac{nRT}{P}$

Boyle's law constant: V = $\frac{\text{constant}}{\text{P}}$

Charles's law: V = constant * T

Avogadro's law: V = constant * n
### The Universal Gas Constant (R)
$$
\text{R} = 0.0820578 \frac{\text{L atm}}{\text{mol K}} = 8.314472 \frac{\text{m}^3\text{ Pa}}{\text{mol K}} = 8.314472 \frac{\text{J}}{\text{mol K}}
$$
- You need R when calculating one quantity from the others.
- You don't need R when calculating a change in conditions
$$
\frac{P_1 V_1}{n_1 T_1} = \frac{P_2 V_2}{n_2 T_2}
$$
### Example
A sample has a volume of 435 mL at 298 K. What is the temp if that sample changes to a volume of 265 mL (pressure stays constant).
K = 182

$$
\frac{435}{298}= \frac{265}{x}
$$
$$
435x = 265*298 = \frac{265*298}{435}=182
$$

# Ideal Gas Law Rearrangements
- Molar mass
	Since n = $\frac{m}{M}$, PV = $\frac{m}{M}$ RT = M = $\frac{mRT}{PV}$
Where n → # moles, m = mass, M = molar mass
- Density
PV = $\frac{m}{M}$ RT → PM = $\frac{m}{V}$ RT
Since d = $\frac{m}{V}$, PM = dRT → d = $\frac{PM}{RT}$
where d = density, M = molar mass
