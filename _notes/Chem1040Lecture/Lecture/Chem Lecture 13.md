# Redox Reactions
Oxidation-Reduction (Redox) Reactions
- Oxidation is the loss of electrons. The reducing agent loses electrons and is oxidized.
- Reduction is the gain of electrons. The oxidizing agent gains electrons and is reduced.
- A redox reaction involves electron transfer.
- oxidation and reduction occur together.

LEO (the lion) says GER
Lose Electrons = Oxidization
Gain Electrons = Reductions

if x loses electrons it's oxidized, the reducing agent, and increases its oxidation number

## Rules for Assigning an Oxidation Number
1. elemental atom (Na, O_2, Cl_2, etc.): O.N. = 0
2. monoatomic ion: O.N. = ion charge
3. sum of O.N. values equals overall charge (zero for compound; ion charge for polyatomic ion).

### Rules for Specific Atoms or Periodic Table Groups
1. Group 1A(1): ON = +1 in all compounds
2. Group 2A(2): ON = +2 in all compounds
3. Hydrogen: ON = +1 in combination with nonmetals, -1 in combination with metals and boron
4. Fluorine: ON = -1 in all compounds
5. Oxygen ON = -1 in peroxides, ON = -2 in all other compounds (except with F)
6. Group 7A(17): ON = -1 in combination with metals, nonmetals (except O), and other halogens lower in the group
