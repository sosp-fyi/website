## Acid-Base Reactions
- An acid is a substance that produces H $^+$ ions when dissolved in H $_2$ O.
- A base is a substance that produces HO $^-$ ions when dissolved in H $_2$ O.
- An acid-base reaction is also called a neutralization reaction

### Acids and Bases as Electrolytes
Strong acids and strong bases are strong electrolytes. 
Weak acids and weak bases are weak electrolytes.

### General Properties of Acids and Bases
Acids: taste sour, dissolve some metals, cause plant dye to change color
Bases: Taste bitter, are slippery, are corrosive

Three theories that help us to understand the chemistry of acids and bases


| Definition     | Acid                                                    | Base                                                 |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Arrhenius      | dissociate in water to give H $^+$ (H $_3$ O $^+$) ions | Dissociate in water to give HO $^-$ ions             |
| Bronsted-Lowry | species that donate a proton H $^+$                     | species that can accept a proton H $^+$              |
| Lewis          | species that can donate electrons to form a new bond    | species that can accept electrons to form a new bond |

## Bronsted-Lowry Theory
Acid - proton (H $^+$) donor
Base - proton (H $^+$) acceptor

When a weak base and a weak acid solution are combined, the conductivity will increase a lot

## Acid-Base Titrations
- conc. of one solution sued to find conc. of another
- standardized solution of base often added to acid of unknown molarity
- acid-base indicator - different colors in acid and base, used to monitor reaction
- at equivalence point, the mol of H $^+$ from acid = mol of OH $^-$ ion from base
- end point - slight excess of base, indicator changes color permanently
