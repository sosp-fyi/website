# Categories of Electrons
Inner (core) electrons are those an atom has in common with the previous noble gas and any completed transition series.
Outer electrons are those in the highest energy level (highest n value).
Valence electrons are those involved in forming compounds.
- Main group elements → outer shell electrons.
- Transition elements → the outer electrons and any (n - 1)d electrons.

Quantum numbers (n, l, m $_l$, m $_s$) for the highest-energy electron in Na:
3, 0, 0, 1/2

Excited state is when an electron is in a higher orbital than expected.

# Trends in Ionization Energy
Ionization energy (IE) is the energy required for the complete removal of 1 mol of electrons from 1 mol of gaseous atoms or ions.

Atoms with low IE tend to form cations.
Atoms with a high IE tend to form anions (except the noble gases).

Ionization energy tends to decrease down a group and increase across a period.
Trend is opposite of the atomic radius.
## The First Three Ionization Energies of Beryllium
- After the 1st electron goes away, it requires more energy to ionize subsequent electrons.
- Ionizing core electrons require much more energy than valence electrons.
Beryllium has 2 valence electrons, so IE $_3$ is much larger than IE $_2$. 
# Trends in Electron Affinity
Electron Affinity (EA) is the energy change that occurs when 1 mol of electrons is added to 1 mol of gaseous atoms or ions.

Atoms with a low EA tend to form cations.
Atoms with a high EA tend to form anions.

Up and to the right is a higher EA.
# Chemical Behavior Patterns Correlated with IE and EA Values
Reactive nonmetals have high IEs and highly negative EAs - form negative ions in ionic compounds.
Reactive metals have low IEs and slightly negative EAs - form positive ions in ionic compounds.
Noble gases have very high IEs and slightly positive EAs - generally unreactive.
