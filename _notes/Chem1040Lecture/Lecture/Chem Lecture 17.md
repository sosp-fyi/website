$$
125000\text{ J} * \frac{1 \text{ kJ}}{1000\text{ J}}*\frac{1\text{ mol C}}{393.5\text{ kJ}}*\frac{12.011\text{ g C}}{1\text{ mol C}} = 3.82 \text{ g C}
$$

$\Delta$ H° $_{rxn}$ = $\Sigma$ m $\Delta$ H° $_{\text{f(products)}}$ - $\Sigma$ n $\Delta$ H° $_{\text{f(reactants)}}$ 

# Standard Enthalpy of Formation
elements → 1 mole of product (all in standard states)

$\Delta$ H = $\Delta$ H $_f$

# Hess' Law
The enthalpy change for an overall process is the sum of the enthalpy changes of its individual steps.
$\Delta$ H $_{\text{overall}}$ = $\Delta$ H $_1$ + $\Delta$ H $_2$ + … + $\Delta$ H $_n$

### Calculating $\Delta$ H for an Overall Process
- Identify the target equation, the step whose $\Delta$ H is unknown.
- Manipulate each equation with known $\Delta$ H values so that the target amount of each substance is on the correct side of the equation.
- Add the manipulated equations and their resulting $\Delta$ H values to get the target equation and its $\Delta$ H.
