Much we know of the inner workings of atoms comes from their interaction with electromagnetic radiation.

The reciprocal relationship of frequency and wavelength

for light, c = 3.00 × 10 $^8$ m/s

# Light (electromagnetic radiation) is a Wave
## Three Observations Did not Fit the Wave Nature of light
1. Blackbody radiation
2. Photoelectric effect
3. Atomic spectra

Is light a particle too?

### Black Body Radiation and the Quantum Theory of Energy
Any object (including atoms) can emit or absorb only certain quantities of energy.

Energy is quantized; it occurs in fixed quantities, rather than being continuous. Each fixed quantity of energy is called a quantum.

An atom changes its energy state by emitting or absorbing one or more quanta of energy.

$\Delta$ E = $\Delta$ $nhv$ where n can only be a whole number.
Planck's constant: h = 6.626 × 10 $^{34}$ J · s.

### The Photoelectric Effect
E = h $\nu$ = $\frac{hc}{\lambda}$
Monochromatic light on metal causes current to flow.
The light must have a minimum frequency that is different for every metal.
The current flows with no lag time.
Light is a particulate bundle of energy called photons.
