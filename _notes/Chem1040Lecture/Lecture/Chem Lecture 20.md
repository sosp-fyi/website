# The Wave-Particle Duality of Matter and Energy
Matter and Energy are alternate forms of the same entity.
E = mc $^2$
All matter exhibits properties of both particles and waves. Electrons have wave-like motion and therefore have only certain allowable frequencies and energies.

Matter behaves as though it moves in a wave, and the de Broglie wavelength for any particles is given by:
$$
\lambda = \frac{h}{mu}
$$
$\lambda$ = wavelength
h = planks constant
m = mass
u = speed in m/s

# Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle
Lets relate this to the visual of an atom and subatomic particles
It is not possible to know accurately both the position and the momentum of a moving particle (electron) at the same time.

$$
\Delta x m \Delta u ≥ \frac{h}{4\pi}
$$
x = position
$\Delta$ x = uncertainty of position
u = speed
$\Delta$ u = uncertainty of speed

The more accurately we know the speed, the less accurately we know the position, and vice versa.

# The Quantum-Mechanical Model of the Atom
The Schrödinger wave equation allows us to solve for the energy state associated with a particular atomic orbital.

The square of the wave function gives the probability density, a measure of the probability of finding an electron of a particular energy in a particular region of the atom.

Orbitals now, not orbits like Bohr model.

## Bookkeeping: Quantum Numbers and Atomic Orbitals — Finding an Electron's “home address”
An atomic orbital is specified by three quantum numbers.

Terminology:
1. n — gives level or shell — size and energy of the orbital (i.e., how far electron is from the nucleus) — higher number = larger orbital and higher energy
2. l — gives sublevel or subshell — describes what shape the orbital home is
3. m $_l$ — exact orientation of orbital home
