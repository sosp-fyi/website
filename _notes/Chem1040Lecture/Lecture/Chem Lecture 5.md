### Chemical Formulas
* A chemical formula consists of
	* element symbols with
	* numerical subscript
* The chemical formula indicates the
	* type and number of each atom present
	* in the smallest unit of a substance

### Naming Binary Ionic Compounds
all ionic compounds (name and formula) - cation first, anion second
binary ionic compound - both cation and anion are monatomic
cation name - same as metal, many end in -ium
anion name - add -ide to root of the nonmetal name

Example: Calcium ions and bromine ions form calcium bromide
we write: Ca $^{2+}$ Br $^{-}$ --> CaBr $_{2}$ 

Example: If a compound forms from Na $^+$ and S $^{2-}$
Na$^+$ S $^{2-}$ ---> Na $_2$ S $_1$ --> NaS

Example: If a compound forms from Ca $^{2+}$ and S $^{2-}$
Ca $^2$ + S $^2-$ --> Ca $_2$ S $_2$ --> CaS

Don't use prefixes on ionic compounds

### Transition Metals - Roman Numerals and Common Names
The roman numeral tells the charge of the ion

Example: Name Cu $_2$ O (use chemistry deduction)
It is: copper (I) oxide

### Naming Acids
Binary acid - for example, HCl(aq):
hydro- + anion root + -ic + acid
hydro + chlor + ic + acid
hydrochloric acid

Oxoacid names - oxoanions, execpt:
-ate becomes -ic in the acid
-ite becomes -ous in the acid

For example: BrO $_{4}^{-}$ is perbromate
HBrO$_4$ is perbromic acid
IO$_2^-$ is iodite
HIO $_2$ is iodous acid

### The Mole
mole (mol) - amount of a substance containing same number of entities as atoms in exactly 12 g of carbon-12.

1 mole contains 6.022 * 10 $^{23}$ entities
(Avogadro's number, N $_A$)
or: Avogadro's constant = 6.022 * 10 $^{23}$ entities/mol
where entities are commonly atoms, ionic formulas or molecules

### Molar Mass
molar mass (M) - mass per mole of entities (atoms, molecules or formula units).

monatomic elements - molar mass same as atomic mass in grams per mole from the periodic table
e.g. molar mass of Ne = 20.18 g/mol
molecules or compounds - molar mass same as molecular mass or formula mass in grams per mole from the periodic table
