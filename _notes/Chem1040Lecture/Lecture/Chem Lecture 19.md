## The line Spectrum of Hydrogen
Electrically excited H atoms emit a beam of light that is narrowed by a slit and dispersed by a prism to create a line spectrum. 

### Three Series of Spectral Lines of Atomic Hydrogen
Rydberg equation = $\frac{1}{\lambda}$ = R ($\frac{1}{n^{2}_{f}}$ - $\frac{1}{n^{2}_{f}}$)
Rydberg constant R = 1.096776 × 10 $^7$ m $^{-1}$
for the visible series, n $_1$ = 2 and n $_2$ = 3,4,5

### Limitations of the Bohr Model
- The Bohr model accurately predicts line spectral emissions of atoms for H only.
- It is a one-electron model ONLY!
	- H (Z=1), He^+ (Z=2), Li^(2+) (Z=3), and Be^(3+) (Z=4)
- Fails for atoms with more electrons due to electron-electron repulsions and additional electron-nucleus attractions.
