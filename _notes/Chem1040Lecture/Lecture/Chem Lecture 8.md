What if you don't add things together in the perfect ratio described by the balanced chemical equation?
You have a limiting reagent (reactant). The thing that runs out first. Anything left over is the excess.

### Reaction Yields

theoretical yield - amount of product calculated from balanced equation - based on the limiting reactant.

actual yield - amount of product obtained (usually less than the theoretical yield).

% yield = $\frac{\text{actual yield}}{\text{theoretical yield}}$ \* 100%

The effect of side reactions on the yield of the main product. Reactions usually make a side product which makes less main product.
