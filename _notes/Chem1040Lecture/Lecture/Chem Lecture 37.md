# Postulates of the Kinetic-Molecular Theory
Postulate 1: Gas particles have negligible volume.
Postulate 2: Gas particles are in constant, random, straight-line motion.
Postulate 3: Particle collisions are elastic (E $_k$ = constant).

We saw "laws" (the math) to summarize observed behavior - this is a model (theory) to explain the behavior.
# How Does Molar Mass Affect Molecular Speed?
At a given temperature, a gas with a lower molar mass (number in parentheses) has a higher most probable speed (peak curve).

Higher mass - lower speed - 
$$
u_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}
$$
"root-mean-square" speed

R = 8.314 $\frac{\text{J}}{\text{mol K}}$ = 8.314 $\frac{\text{kg m }^2}{\text{s}^2\text{ mol K}}$
T = K
M = $\frac{\text{kg}}{\text{mol}}$
# Graham's Law of Effusion
Effusion - a gas escapes through a small hole into an evacuated space.

Graham's law of effusion - rate inversely proportional to the square root of molar mass
A lighter gas effuses more quickly than a heaver gas. 

rate $\propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{M}}$

### Graham's Law
$$
\frac{\text{rate}_A}{\text{rate}_B} = \sqrt{\frac{M_B}{M_A}}= \frac{\text{time}_B}{\text{time}_A}
$$
# Real Gases: Deviations from Ideal Behavior
- The kinetic-molecular model - ideal gases. Real gases deviate.
- Real gas particles have real volume.
- Real gas particles attract and repel each other.
- Real gases deviate most at low temperature and high pressure.
# The Van Der Waals Equation (equation of the state)
- The van der waals equation adjusts the ideal gas law to take into account
	- The real volume of the gas particles and 
	- The effect of interparticle attractions

Van der Waals equation for n moles of a real gas:
$$
(P + \frac{n^2a}{V^2})(V-nb) = nRT
$$
The constant a relates to factors that influence the attraction between particles. a for "attraction".
The constant b relates to particle volume. b for "bulk".
