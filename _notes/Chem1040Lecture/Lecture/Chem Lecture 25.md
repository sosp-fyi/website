# Lewis Electron-Dot Symbols
Lewis symbol for a main-group element:
- Element symbol - nucleus & core electrons.
- A-group (main group elements) number - number of valence electrons.
- One dot on each side of symbol.
- Remaining dots, paired.

# The Octet Rule
Elements lose, gain, or share electrons when bonding in order to have a filled outer shell with 8 electrons.
- True for nearly all of period 2, and many other elements.
- Exceptions: H and He have 2 electrons in outer shell → Doublet Rule.
# Periodic Trends in Lattice Energy
Lattice Energy - energy it takes to break apart the ionic solid (how well is that solid sticking together).
Lattice energy is affected by ionic size and ionic charge.
As ionic sizes increases, lattice energy decreases.
As ionic charges increases, lattice energy increases.

# Making Pictures of Covalent Bonding
## Bonding Pairs and Lone Pairs
Share to get full outer level - shared electrons called shared pair or bonding pair.

Shared pair shown as a pair of dots or line:
H:H or H-H
outer-level electron pair not bonding: lone pair, or unshared paired.
# Properties of a Covalent Bond
Bond Order - the number of electron pairs being shared by a pair of atoms.
Bond Energy (BE) - the energy needed to overcome the attraction between the nuclei and the shared electrons. The stronger the bond the higher the bond energy.
Bond Length - the distance between the nuclei of the bonded atoms.
