# Valence-Shell Electron-Pair Repulsion Theory (VSEPR)
Each group of valence electrons around a central atom is located as far as possible from the others, to minimize repulsions.

A "group" of electrons is any number of electrons that occupies a localized region around an atom.

"Group": can be a single bond, double bond, triple bond, lone pair, or even a single electron.
# Electron-group Arrangement vs. Molecular Shape
The electron-group arrangement is defined by both bonding and non-bonding electron groups.

The molecular shape is the three-dimensional arrangement of nuclei joined by the bonding groups. This is defined by the relative positions of the nuclei.

Molecular shape is classified using the designation:

AX $_m$ E $_n$
A → central atom
X → surrounding atom
E → non-bonding valence-electron group
m and n are integers.

BrF $_5$ is AX $_5$ E 
# Bond Angle
The bond angle is the angle formed by the nuclei of two surrounding atoms with the nucleus of the central atom.

The angles shown are ideal bond angles, determined by basic geometry alone. Real bond angles deviate from the ideal value in many cases.

# Molecular Shape and Molecular Polarity
Overall molecular polarity depends on both shape and bond polarity.

The polarity of a molecule is measured by its dipole moment ($\mu$), which is given in the unit debye (D).

A molecule is polar if
- It contains one or more polar bonds.
- The individual bond dipoles do not cancel.
Example: CO $_2$
