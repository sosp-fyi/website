# Gases and the Kinetic-Molecular Theory
The three states of matter: gas, liquid, and solid
# Gas Pressure and Its Measurement
Pressure = $\frac{\text{force}}{\text{area}}$
Atmospheric pressure arises from the force exerted by atmospheric gases on the Earth's surface.
Atmospheric pressure decreases with altitude.
# Behavior of Gases
- The physical behavior of gases is defined by:
	- Pressure (P)
	- Temperature (T)
	- Volume (V)
	- Amount (number of moles, n)
- Variables are interdependent
- Relationships described by the gas laws.

Robert Boyle (1627-1691)
Changes in pressure and volume of a gas are inversely proportional
P $_1$ V $_1$ = P $_2$ V $_2$

Jacques Charles (1746-1823)
Temperature and volume are proportional
$\frac{V_1}{T_1}$ = $\frac{V_2}{T_2}$ 

Absolute zero (0 K) is the temperature at which an ideal gas would have a zero volume.

Amonton's Law
P = T or P = constant x T
$\frac{P_1}{T_1}$ = $\frac{P_2}{T_2}$

Avogadro
$\frac{V_1}{n_1}$ = $\frac{V_2}{n_2}$
# Standard Temperature and Pressure
(STP) 0C, 1 atm
# Ideal Gas Law
PV = nRT or V = $\frac{nRT}{P}$
The universal gas constant (R):
R = 0.0820578 $\frac{\text{L atm}}{\text{mol K}}$
