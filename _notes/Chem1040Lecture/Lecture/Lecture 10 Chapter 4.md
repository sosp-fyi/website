## Definitions

Solution - A mixture that has no visible boundaries among its components (i.e. homogeneous mixture)
Solute - A smaller quantity of a substance dissolved in a larger quantity of another
Solvent - A larger quantity substance; for aqueous solutions, solvent is water

Electron distribution in H $_2$ and H $_2$ O
A. electron charge distribution in H $_2$ is symmetrical.
B. Electron charge distribution in H $_2$ O is asymmetrical
C. Each bond in H $_2$ O is polar
D. The whole H $_2$ O molecule is polar

The electrical conductivity of ionic solutions
A. Distilled water does not conduct a current.
B. Positive and negative ions fixed in a solid do not conduct a current.
C. In solution, positive and negative ions move and conduct a current.

The more ions in a solution the greater the conductivity.

Electrolytes

- Dissolve in water to form a conductive solution
- Strong electrolytes dissociate completely
- Weak electrolytes dissociate only partially
  strong acid or base = strong electrolyte
  weak acid or base = weak electrolyte

Solution Stoichiometry

- Many reactions occur in solution.
- A solution consists of one or more solutes dissolved in a solvent.
- The concentrations of a solution is given by the quantity of solute present in a given quantity of solution.
- Molarity (M) is often used to express concentration

Molarity = $\frac{\text{moles solute}}{\text{liters of solution}}$ M = $\frac{\text{mol solute}}{\text{L of solution}}$

To prepare 250 mL of saline solution .15 M NaCl we need ? g NaCl (58.44 g/mol)

$$
250 \text{ mL} * \frac{1 \text{ L}}{1000 \text{ mL}} * \frac{0.150 \text{ mol}}{1 \text{ L}}* \frac{58.44 \text{ g}}{1 \text{ mol}} = 2.19 \text{ g NaCl}
$$

Want 755 mL of .0125 M CH_3OH(aq) solution. How many mL liquid CH_3OH needed?
(d=.7918 g/mL, M=32.04 g/mol)

$$
755 \text{ mL}*\frac{1\text{ L}}{1000\text{ mL}}*\frac{.0125\text{ mols}}{1\text{ L}}*\frac{32.04\text{ g}}{1\text{ mols}}*\frac{1\text{ mols}}{.7918 \text{ g}} = .382 \text{ mL}
$$
