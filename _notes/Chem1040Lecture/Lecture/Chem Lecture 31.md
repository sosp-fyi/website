# Molecular Orbital (MO) Theory and Valence Bond (VB) Theory
A new/different way to look at molecules?
Yes! A model is only as good as its limitations.
VSEPR is awesome at predicting shapes but doesn't say anything about orbitals.
So let's get describing orbitals back into our model!
# Valence Bond Theory
Basic principle:
- Covalent bond - overlap of orbitals of two atoms, with a pair of electrons occupying the overlap region.
The number of hybrid orbitals formed equals the number of atomic orbitals mixed.
The type of hybrid orbitals formed varies with the types of atomic orbitals mixed.
The shape and orientation of a hybrid orbital maximizes overlap with the other atom in the bond.

# Types of Covalent Bonds
A sigma ($\sigma$) bond is formed by end-to-end overlap of orbitals.

All single bonds are $\sigma$ bonds.
A pi ($\pi$) bond is formed by sideways overlap of two p orbitals.

A double bond consists of one $\sigma$ bond and one $\pi$ bonds
A triple bond consists of one $\sigma$ bond and two $\pi$ bonds
