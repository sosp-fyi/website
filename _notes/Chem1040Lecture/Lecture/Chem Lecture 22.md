The quantum numbers n and l are associated with the energy of an electron in a many-electron atom.
# How Do You Know what Orbital Has the Lowest Energy?
## The Effect of Nuclear charge (Z $_{\text{effective}}$)
Higher nuclear charge lowers orbital energy (stabilizes the system) by increasing nucleus-electron attractions.
## The Effect of Electron Repulsions (Shielding)
An additional electron in the same orbital raises the orbital energy through electron-election repulsions.
Inner electrons shield outer electrons more effectively than do electrons in the same sublevel. Additional inner electrons raise orbital energy and lower the Z $_{\text{eff}}$ felt by outer electrons due to shielding.

Greater nuclear charge lowers sublevel energy.
