# The Steps in Converting a Molecular Formula into a Lewis Structure
- Step 1 Molecular Formula: place atom with the lowest EN in center.
- Step 2 Atom Placement: add A group numbers.
- Step 3 Sum of Valence e $^-$: Draw single bonds, and subtract 2e $^-$ for each bond.
- Step 4 Remaining Valence e $^-$: Give each atom 8e $^-$ (2e for H) OCTET rule
- Step 5 Lewis Structure: If central atom lacks octet, move lone e $^-$ to make bonding pair

Key checks:
1. Correct # of valence electrons drawn
2. Octet rule followed

Good to remember:
H → one bond
N → three bonds
C → four bonds
O → two bonds
Halogen → one bond if it is a surrounding (not central) atom - F is always a surrounding atom.
### Sometimes
1. More than one "central" atom, e.g., C $_2$ H $_5$ OH
2. "Run out" of valence electrons, e.g., HCN
3. More than one structure possible. Call this resonance forms, e.g., NO $_{2}^{-}$
## If the Resonance Forms Are not Equivalent, how Do We Choose Which is the Best?
We use formal charges
# Formal Charges
Each bonded atom is assigned half of the shared electrons.
Each atom is a bond is assigned all of its unshared electrons.

FC = Ve $^-$ - 1/2 be $^-$ - nbe $^-$

FC = Formal charge
