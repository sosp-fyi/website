2. A compound is 70.6% C, 5.9% H, and 23.5% O by mass. Its molar mass is between 100 and 140 g/mol. What is its molecular formula?
C = $\frac{70.6}{12.011}=5.878$

C = $\frac{5.878}{1.469}=4$

### Features of Chemical Equations

| Mg + O $_2$           | →                                               | MgO                            |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Reactants on the left | A yield arrow points from reactants to products | Products, written on the right |
* The equation must be balanced, meaning that the same number and type of each atom must appear on both sides.
* The balanced equation shows the atomic ratio and molar ratio of reactants to products.

### Balancing a Chemical Equation
Mg + O $_2$ → MgO
2Mg + O $_2$ → 2MgO

2Mg(s) + O $_2$ (g) → 2MgO(s)

Example:
