### Compound Mass into Moles and Molecules
How many moles are represented by $4.63 * 10 ^{24}$ formula units of sodium nitrate?
$$
4.63*10^{24} \text{ units NaNO}_3 * \frac{1 \text{ mol NaNO}_3}{6.022*10^{23} \text{ units NaNO}_3} = 7.69 \text{ mol NaNO}_3
$$

What is the mass of $3.7*10^{21}$ formula units of sodium nitrate
$$
3.7*10^{21} \text{ units NaNO}_3 * \frac{1 \text{ mol}}{6.022*10^{23} \text{ units NaNO}_3} * \frac{85.00 \text{ g}}{1 \text{ mol}} = .52 \text{ g NaNO}_3
$$

### Steps to Find Molecular Formula
1. Assume that 100. g of the compound was analyzed. 
	* The mass % data can then be treated as actual masses.
2. Convert the mass of each element into moles, using the atomic mass as a conversion factor.
3. Find the smallest whole-number ratio of the elements to get the empirical formula.
4. Calculate $\frac {\text{molecular mass}}{\text {empirical formula mass}}$ and multiply the subscripts in the empirical formula by this number.

Example: What is the formula of a compound that is composed of 41.39% carbon, 3.47% hydrogen and 55.14% oxygen and has a molar mass of 116.07 g/mol?
C = $41.39 \text{ g C} * \frac{1 \text{ mol C}}{12.01 \text{ g C}} = 3.446 \text{ mol C}$
H = $3.47 \text{ g H} * \frac{1 \text{ mol H}}{1.008 \text{ g H}} = 3.442 \text{ mol H}$ 
O = $55.14 \text{ g O} * \frac{1 \text{ mol O}}{16.00 \text{ g O}} = 3.446 \text{ mol O}$ 

$\frac {3.446}{3.442} = 1.00$
$\frac {3.442}{3.442} = 1.00$
$\frac {3.446}{3.442} = 1.00$
Therefore, the formula is CHO for the empirical formula.

Empirical formula mass = $12.011 + 1.008 + 16.00 = 29.02$ g/mol
$$
\frac {\text{molecular mass}}{\text {empirical formula mass}} = \frac{116.07 \text{ g/mol}}{29.02 \text{ g/mol}} = 3.9996 => 4.0 
$$
The molecular formula is C $_4$ H $_4$ O $_4$

## Solving for Molecular Formula from Mass Percentages
Example: An unknown hydrocarbon is analyzed and found to be 82.63% carbon and the rest hydrogen. It is also determined that the molar mass of this compound is 58.14 g/mol. What is the molecular formula of this compound?
82.63 g C
17.37 g H
$82.63/12.01 = 6.880$ mol C
$17.37/1.008 = 17.23$ mol H
$\frac{6.880}{6.880} = 1$
$\frac{17.23}{6.880} = 2.5$
$2(12.01) + 5(1.008) = 29.06$
$\frac{58.14}{29.06} = 2$

### Interpreting a Chemical Equation
Example: What information is provided by the balanced chemical equation 
2Al (s) + 3Cl $_2$ (g) → 2AlCl $_3$ (s)? 

* Al (s) and Cl $_2$ (g) are reactants
	* Al is a solid and Cl $_2$ is a gas.
* AlCl $_3$ (s) is a product
	* This substance is a solid

* Al is an element composed of atoms
* Cl $_2$ is an element composed of molecules
* AlCl $_3$ is a compound composed of individual ions
	* AlCl $_3$ is the formula for one formula unit of this compound

2Al (s) + 3Cl $_2$ (g) → 2AlCl $_3$ (s)
2 Al atoms react with 3 Cl $_2$ molecules to form 2 formula units of AlCl $_3$ 

2 moles of Al react with 3 moles of Cl $_2$ to form 2 moles of AlCl $_3$ 

53.96 g of Al react with 212.7 g of Cl $_2$ to form 133.3 g of AlCl $_3$ 

### Key Points in Balancing Equations
* A coefficient applies to all atoms in the formula that follows it.

* Do not change a chemical formula by altering subscripts.

### Limiting Reagent
The reactant in a chemical reaction that is completely consumed first, thereby limiting the amount of product that can be formed.

### Finding the Limiting Reagent
One industrial process for producing nitric acid begins with the following reaction:

4 NH $_3$ + 5 O $_2$ → 4 NO + 6 H $_2$ O

What mass of NO is formed in the reaction of 469 g of NH $_3$ and 2080 g O $_2$?

$$
469 \text{ g NH}_3 * \frac{1 \text{ mol NH}_3}{17.03 \text{ g NH}_3} * \frac{4 \text{ mol NO}}{4 \text{ mol NH}_3} * \frac{30.01 \text{ g NO}}{1 \text{ mol NO}} = 826 \text{ g NO}
$$
$$
2080 \text{ g O}_2 * \frac{1 \text{ mol O}_2}{32 \text{ g O}_2} * \frac{4 \text{ mol NO}}{5 \text{ mol O}_2} * \frac{30.01 \text{ g NO}}{1 \text{ mol NO}} = 1560 \text{ g NO}
$$
NH $_3$ is the limiting reactant and 826 g of NO will be produced
