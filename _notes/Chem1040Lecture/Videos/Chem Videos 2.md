### Calculating Mass Fractions
$$
\text{mass fraction of element} = \frac{\text{mass of element in compound}}{\text{mass of compound}}
$$
$$
\text{mass percent of element} = \frac{\text{mass of element in compound}}{\text{mass of compound}}*100
$$
Example: Calculate the mass fraction of oxygen in water if 45.05 g of H $_{2}$ O contains 40.00 g of oxygen.
Solution:
mass fraction of O = $\frac{\text{mass of O}}{\text{mass of H}_{2}\text{O}}$ 
$= \frac{40.00 g}{45.05 g}$
$= 0.8879$
mass percent is 88.79%

Example: A 16.0 g sample of CH $_{4}$ contains 12.0g of carbon. How much hydrogen is in a 75.0 g sample of CH $_{4}$?
Solution:
$$
75.0\text{ g CH}_4 * \frac{4\text{ g H}}{16\text{ g CH}_4} = 18.8 \text{ g H}
$$$$
75-18.8 = 56.2 \text{ g C}
$$
