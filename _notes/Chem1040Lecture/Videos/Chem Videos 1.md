### Temperature Conversion
Conversion from Celsius to Kelvin is 
$$
K = C^{\circ} + 273.15
$$
Celsius to Fahrenheit is
$$
^{\circ}F = 1.8(^{\circ}C) + 32
$$
Fahrenheit to Celsius is
$$
^{\circ}C = \frac{^{\circ}F - 32}{1.8}
$$

### Significant Figures
* Start at the left of the number and move right until you reach the first nonzero digit. Count all following digits as significant.
Example: 0.0==456== has 3 significant digits.
* Zeros between other digits are always significant
Example: ===120.3=== has 4 significant digits.
* Zeros at the right of the number are always significant as long as there is a decimal point present.
Example: ==2.0== has 2 significant digits.

For numbers without a decimal point:
* Zeros at the end of the number are **not** considered significant.

For example, ==23==00 has only **2 significant figures**. If these zeros are known with certainty, the measurement should be recorded as ==2300==. or $2.300\; x\; 10^3$.

==24==0 has 2 significant digits, but ==240.== has 3 significant digits.

### Rounding a Number Ending in 5
A special rule for rounding is applied when the digit to be removed is a 5 with no other digits following it.
* If the last desired digit is even, we do not change the value of the digit
Example: 23.==4==5 rounds to 23.4
* If the desired digit is odd, we round up to the next even number.
Example: 15.==7==5 rounds to 15.8

**This rule only applies if there are no other digits following the 5 that is being removed.**
Example: 32.651 rounds to 32.7

### Determining the Significant Figures in a Calculation
Example 1: Convert a distance of 65.2 miles to km, given that 1 mi = 1.609 km.

Solution:
$$
65.2 \text{ mi x } \frac{1.609 \text{ km}}{1 \text{ mi}} = 104.9068 \text{ km} = 105 \text{ km}
$$

Example 2: Simplify the following expression and round to the correct number of significant digits:
$$
\frac{0.045 + 1.23}{0.735}
$$
Solution:
$$
\frac{0.045 + 1.23}{0.735} = \frac{1.28}{0.735}
$$
$$
\frac{1.28}{0.735}=1.74
$$

### Converting between Metric and Nonmetric Units
Example: Convert a speed of 25 mph to units of m/s, if 1 mi = 1609 m.

Solution:
25 mph means the same as $\frac{25 \text{ mi}}{1\text{ h}}$.

$$
\frac{25 \text{ mi}}{1\text{ h}}*\frac{1609\text{ m}}{1\text{ mi}}*\frac{1\text{ h}}{60\text{ min}}*\frac{1\text{ min}}{60\text{ s}} = 11 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}} \text{ or 11 m/s}
$$

### Solving Volume given Density and Mass
Example: A solution with a density of 1.71 g/mL has a mass of 12.1 grams. What is the volume in mL?

Solution:
Density = 1.71 g/mL = $\frac{1.71\text{ g}}{1\text{ mL}}$ 
$$
12.1 \text{ g } * \frac{1\text{ mL}}{1.71\text{ g}} = 7.08 \text{ mL}
$$
$12.1 / 1.71 = 7.076$ rounds to $7.08$ mL

### Interpreting Information in Mathematical Terms
Example: The density of aluminum is 2.7 g/cm $^3$. A student has collected 126 empty aluminum cans for recycling. If there are 21 cans per pound, what volume of aluminum (in cm $^3$) has the student collected? 1 kg = 2.205 lb.

Solution:
$\frac{126}{21} = 6$
$\frac{6}{2.205} = 2.721$
$2.721*1000 = 2721.088$
$\frac{2721.088}{2.7} = 1007.81$ or 1.0 x 10 $^3$ cm $^3$

or
$$
126\text{ cans } * \text{ }\frac{1\text{ lb}}{21\text{ cans}} \text{ } * \text{ } \frac{1000\text{ g}}{2.205\text{ lb}} \text{ } * \text{ } \frac{1 \text{ cm}^3}{2.7\text{ g}} = 1.0 \text{ x } 10^3 \text{ cm}^3
$$
