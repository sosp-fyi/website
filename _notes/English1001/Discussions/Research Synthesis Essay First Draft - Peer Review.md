# First Peer Review
## Thesis & Purpose
Thesis is very clear. Social media influencers play a large role in affecting what people buy, through perception of products and perceived trendiness. The essay is only informational, it doesn't try to persuade the audience into thinking one thing or another. Uses the sources to prove the points being presented. 
## Organization & Flow
All paragraphs are ordered by themes/trends rather than by source. They go in the order of psychological factors, social mechanisms, informational roles, quantitative effects, and long-term impacts. Paragraphs 2-4 flow well, but maybe consider merging the "Quantitative results reveal" paragraph into a previous one. Stronger transition phrases at the beginning of paragraphs would help as well. 
## Synthesis & Use of Sources
All sources provided are scholarly and peer-reviewed. All body paragraphs include at least two of the sources for each point. There are quantitative and qualitative findings in all paragraphs (or at least point to sources which show that). A good example of quantitative data being shown in the draft is in paragraph four 
## Evidence & Citation
All sources provided are scholarly and peer-reviewed. The third through sixth paragraph use just page numbers for citations instead of (author pg. #). Paragraph four, sentence two, doesn't have a citation when showing the statistical evidence for influencers behavior. Almost all citations are paraphrased and not direct quotes as well.
## Clarity, Tone & Style
Slight mistake in the first paragraph where it says "in addition to being entertainers due to their high growth of their platforms" chained theirs make it hard to read this sentence. Try to find some synonyms for "their". The common and consistent in this sentence don't work well with each other, removing one will increase clarity "The most common consistent finding within the research". Try not to use "influence" and "influencer" in the same sentence for better readability (this is seen in the last sentence of the second paragraph).
## Conclusion & Implications
The conclusion restates the main themes and explicitly mentions research gaps. This is explicitly said in the conclusion, where it states that the effects of AI-generated influencers haven't been studied yet and that is another avenue for further studies.
## Works Cited Page
Formatting is ok on the Works Cited page. There are parts that are in all caps and untranslated, which is wrong. The link to the third source doesn't work, and it doesn't seem to have an author listed. 
## Strengths
The best part about this essay is the strength of the topic being presented. By having strong thematic paragraphs, the purpose behind the essay is enhanced, which works very well. All aspects of the essay contribute to provide reasons behind influencers effects on people and their buying habits. 
## Top Revision Priorities
1. Strengthen synthesis in paragraphs 3 and 4 by explicitly comparing and contrasting studies instead of summarizing them in sequence.
2. Revise the thesis into a single, unified sentence that clearly previews the main themes.
3. Fix MLA Works Cited formatting, especially author names and title capitalization for the third entry.
4. Improve clarity and concision by revising repeated words or awkward sentences (examples provided above).
5. Enhance transitions between paragraphs to further emphasize how themes build on each other.

# Second Peer Review
## Thesis & Purpose
The thesis states that services improve student well-being but remain limited by access, stigma, and usability, which is clear but split across multiple sentences. It stays informative, not persuasive. A single unified thesis sentence would strengthen clarity.
## Organization & Flow
The essay is organized by themes, historical trends, service accessibility, and academic impact. It doesn't summarize sources one by one. The transitions between paragraphs are logical and provide a good flow (e.g., "Another finding across the research is..." and "The relationship between mental health and academic performance is reinforced..."). No paragraphs feel out of place.  
## Synthesis & Use of Sources
Each body paragraph includes at least two sources to support all points. For example, paragraph 5 connects Bower's older observations on emotional distress with Duffy's modern quantitative findings on anxiety and academic damage, showing how they reinforce the same idea over time. All sources are scholarly. 
## Evidence & Citation
All citations are paraphrased rather than direct quotes. However, some sentences seem to be missing citations such as, "Although Guo’s study focuses on school-based health centers meant for K-12 students, their analysis of medicaid data shows that students with mental health problems who used SBHCs had significantly lower total healthcare costs, including reduced hospitalizations". The citations, where present, follow MLA style with (author page). Again, all sources are scholarly.
## Clarity, Tone & Style
The writing maintains a clear, objective, and academic tone throughout. Most of the writing is strong, but a few sentences could be revised for clarity and concision. The sentence "By viewing the trends in mental service use, the effects of school-based care, and the changing views of student mental health." is a fragment. It should be combined with the thesis sentence that follows it. In the thesis, the phrase "service usability limits their full impact" is slightly awkward. It would be clearer as "service usability _that limits_ their full impact". Be sure to use "who" when referring to people instead of "that" (e.g., "students _who_ are struggling" and "people _who_ used the SBHC").
## Conclusion & Implications
The conclusion effectively synthesizes the main patterns identified in the essay, recapping the historical context, the benefits of integrated services, and the link to academic success. It does a good job of re-emphasizing the "persistent gaps" (stigma, lack of capacity) as the key takeaway. The final sentence, "As mental health concerns continue to increase among college students, the need for accessible, effective, and well rounded mental health services is more urgent now than ever", serves as a strong implication drawn from the research.
## Works Cited Page
The Works Cited page is present, correctly alphabetized, however, it does not correctly use hanging indentation. Also, the first sources DOI isn't correctly linked and is bringing up an error page. 
## Strengths
The strongest element of this draft is the synthesis. You consistently put sources in conversation with each other to build thematic arguments. Your ability to connect Bower's 1962 article with Duffy's 2019 article to show a long-term, consistent trend is excellent. The organization and clarity of the argument are also major strengths.
## Top Revision Priorities
1. Correct the sentence fragment in the introduction. Combine "By viewing the trends..." with the thesis sentence that follows to create a complete, complex sentence.
2. Refine the thesis wording for grammatical smoothness. Change "...stigma, and service usability limits their full impact" to "...stigma, and service usability _that limits_ their full impact."
3. Proofread for minor grammatical errors. Specifically, change "that" to "who" when referring to people, and check for any missing verbs (e.g., "Klerman’s earlier assessments _show_ that...").

# Third Peer Review, Cause Fuck Me. Right?
## Thesis & Purpose
The thesis is clear, specific, and identifies three central themes: evolving youth-oriented genres, identity formation through global media, and nostalgia shaping lifelong preferences (end of paragraph 1). It successfully maintains an informative purpose rather than a persuasive one. However, the thesis sentence is long and could be simplified into one streamlined statement to improve clarity. The focus remains consistent throughout the essay, and each body paragraph supports one of the stated themes.
## Organization & Flow
The essay is organized thematically, with each body section clearly tied to one of the three central themes. Paragraphs 2 through 4 have strong internal flow, but paragraph 3 begins more like a summary of technological change rather than tying immediately back to the sources, making it read slightly isolated. Some transitions between major sections, particularly between paragraphs 4 and 5, could be smoother to reinforce the shift from nostalgia to globalization. Reordering a few sentences within paragraphs 3 and 6 could also improve logical progression.
## Synthesis & Use of Sources
Most major points integrate at least two scholarly sources, especially paragraphs 2, 4, and 6. Paragraph 3 relies more heavily on description than synthesis, pairing the technological shift discussion more directly with the findings from Soto-Sanfiel et al. would strengthen the argument. Quantitative and qualitative findings appear throughout, though quantitative examples (such as the sample size of 937 students) could be connected more directly to conclusions. All sources used are scholarly, peer-reviewed journal articles, meeting requirements.
## Evidence & Citation
Evidence is mostly paraphrased effectively, with very few direct quotes. MLA citations are present, but a few issues appear, for example, paragraph 3 includes no citation for the claims about online discussion boards and streaming access. All in-text citations appear to use parenthetical author-page format correctly. Works Cited entries are mostly complete, though Schindler is misspelled as “Schidler,” and the italics/indentation formatting needs minor cleanup.
## Clarity, Tone & Style
The writing maintains an academic and objective tone, but some sentences are overly long and could be broken for clarity, for example, the final sentence of paragraph 1 contains multiple clauses that compete for emphasis. A few places use slightly repetitive wording (e.g., “medium” appears multiple times in close succession in paragraph 2). Grammar is generally strong, though paragraph 4 has a minor subject-verb clarity issue in “Schindler and Holbrook’s illicit that preference patterns…” where “illicit” should be “elicit.” Overall readability is high but could be improved with concision.
## Conclusion & Implications
The conclusion effectively synthesizes recurring themes and clearly articulates future research directions, especially regarding online algorithms and short-form media. It restates the central arguments without repeating wording from earlier paragraphs. One improvement would be explicitly connecting these future directions back to the scholarly gaps identified by the sources themselves. The final sentences provide a strong sense of the broader implications of cinema’s cultural impact.
## Works Cited Page
All required entries are present and include DOIs or URLs, which appear functional, except for the first sources DOI which brings gives an error page. There are several formatting issues: “Schidler” should be corrected to “Schindler,” titles need proper title capitalization, and some entries have inconsistent punctuation. There shouldn't be any spaces after each source as well. Otherwise, it adheres to MLA 9 guidelines.
## Strengths
The strongest element of the draft is its clear thematic structure, which makes the complex topic of youth and cinema easy to follow. The synthesis between nostalgia psychology, globalization, and film culture is compelling and shows a deep understanding of the research. Additionally, the writing demonstrates strong control of tone and maintains academic rigor throughout.
## Top Revision Priorities
1. Revise and condense the thesis into one clear, unified sentence that previews the main themes more concisely.
2. Improve synthesis in paragraph 3 by directly connecting technological shifts to the scholarly findings (e.g., Soto-Sanfiel et al.).
3. Fix MLA errors: correct “Schidler” to “Schindler,” check capitalization, and remove unnecessary spaces in Works Cited page.
4. Add missing citations in paragraph 3 and clarify sentences that are overly long or repetitive.
5. Strengthen transitions between major sections, especially between paragraphs 4–5 and 5–6.
