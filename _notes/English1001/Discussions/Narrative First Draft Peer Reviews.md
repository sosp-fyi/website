# First Peer-Review
The thesis statement is implicit and very clear. Through storytelling and reflection, the theme of the essay is shown. This essay also does a good job of connecting the personal story to a meaningful lesson. The focus on why this memory matters to you till this day gives the essay a strong sense of purpose.  
  
The introduction immediately grabs my attention by describing the freeing feeling of skiing, which later contrasts with the accident. This creates a strong hook and a desire to find out what happens. The essay is formatted in chronological order, which helps the reader follow the events clearly. However, some paragraphs are quite long and could be condensed to improve the flow and readability.  
  
The essay uses strong sensory details. For example, the icy slopes, the rippling wind and the pain in your shoulder. There is also some spoken dialog and lots of internal dialogue, which adds to the emotion and immersion in the story. I was emotionally connected to the story. I think that you did a good job of fully explaining the situation, but the second paragraph is significantly longer than all the others. While it is nice to know the full depth of the story, it could definitely be condensed.  
  
The third paragraph does a good job explaining why the experience matters and ties back to the thesis. However, there is little done to connect your experience with the audience. After that is added, your essay will make the reader more empathetic. This will subsequently strengthen your thesis because the reader will connect your experience with something in their own life. Overall, the reflection ties back to the thesis.  
  
There are currently some issues with the MLA format. Mainly the paragraph formatting, but this can easily be fixed by taking a look at the template MLA doc on canvas. There are a few issues with grammar, punctuation and spelling. I'd recommend using Word or a spell checker to fix these issues. Also, there are quite a few run-on sentences, so I'd recommend reading it back over and splitting those long sentences into two or three separate ones. Overall, it isn't that bad. Use a spell checker and focus on the sentence structure, and it'll be a lot more polished.  
  
The strongest part of the draft is the vivid details and in-depth story-telling. While the second paragraph is long due to this, it can easily be condensed while keeping the gist of the story the same. Some areas that you could work on are your sentence structure and grammar. Breaking up long sentences, correcting capitalization and increasing word variation (i.e., not using the same word multiple times in a single sentence) will make your essay more polished.  
  
# Second Peer-Review
The thesis for this essay is implicit and very clear and well-defined. The essay connects the personal story to a greater lesson. That being: knowing when to give up for the sake of your mental health, even when you're giving up something you love.  
  
The introduction is instantly captivating, with a strong hook and an interesting thesis. The flow is straightforward to follow and sets up the following paragraphs with smooth transitions. The essay is set up in a logical format moving from the writer's identity being tied to volleyball, to their breaking point, making the decision to quit and finally the reflection on the broader life lesson. The progression feels natural and logical.  
  
There are a plethora of sensory details, especially during the “city game” scene where they describe the squeaking of the shoes and the crowd yelling, etc. The spoken dialogue with the parents adds more emotional depth because it shows different perspectives. There is also internal dialogue that helps the reader connect to the writer's mindset. I felt emotionally connected to this story and the themes being described, especially because I can somewhat relate to the situation.  
  
The reflection is one of the strongest parts of the essay. The writer clearly explains why the experience matters to them and ties it back to the thesis: that letting go of something you love can be a good thing if it's doing more harm than good. This lesson is widely applicable and isn't something limited to athletes.  
  
The essay follows the MLA format perfectly. There are some very minor grammatical errors. A few run-on sentences should be split into two (e.g., “My worth was built on every spike, block and set I had done, however, I met a reality”). Overall, the essay was well written with very minuscule mistakes that can be edited for the final version.  
  
Overall, the strongest part of the draft is the emotion described and shown, through sensory details, throughout the story. One specific area that could be improved is reducing the repetition in the reflection sections. There are repeated mentions of “true strength is walking away,” these could be condensed. In their place, a few more details about life after volleyball could be added to make the conclusion even stronger.
