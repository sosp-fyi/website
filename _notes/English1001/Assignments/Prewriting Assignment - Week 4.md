# Prewriting and Brainstorming Worksheet
  
1. Preparing to Write a Narrative Argument Think of an event that shaped who you are and taught you a lesson that could also teach others. Write down the event in a few sentences. This will be the starting point for your narrative argument essay.  

Event description: 

At my career tech high school, they offered a plethora of certifications that we could take for free. I took advantage of this and completed all of them but one by the last month before summer break in the first year. However, the last one that I had to do I had already taken three times and had failed (you could only take it five times per year). So, I was worried that I wouldn’t complete it before the end of the school year. I would be the first person to complete all of them in one year if I passed this last test. In the end, I did a bunch of studying and passed it, and the feeling of relief from completing my goal was unforgettable. So, the lesson would be to persevere and achieve the goals you set for yourself because the feeling of accomplishment is well worth the effort.  
  
(There is more I could talk about regarding this situation and lessons derived from it, but I don’t know how much I will need to write or what the exact details are for this).  
  
  
2. Directions    
   Below are several prewriting and brainstorming strategies. Choose at least FOUR different methods to complete. Each method will help you think about your event in a new way and prepare you to write your essay.  
  
  
A. Freewriting (5 minutes)    
Write nonstop about your event. Do not worry about grammar or spelling.  
  
This event happened in May 2024. I took the last certification test, on databases, like two weeks before the last day of school. My goal I set for myself was to complete/pass all the certifications offered through my program in the first year I was there. I wanted to do this because I would be the second person to complete all of them, but the first to do them all in a year. After I took the test and found out I passed, I ended up calling my mom because she helped me push through and take the test. I was pretty worried about failing the test again. The time pressure due to the end of the year as well as the thought that I could only take it two more times led me to not want to take it and wait for next year. My mom encouraged me to study for them and take it because I could always do it next year if it didn’t work out. In the end, she was right, and I did pass after a lot of studying, and for that I am grateful.  
  
  
B. Listing List as many thoughts, details, or lessons as you can about the event.  
  
- Persevering and accomplishing your goals give an unmistakable feeling of relief once achieved.
- I took the last test early in the morning, around 9 am on May 9, 2024. The last day of school was around the 18th of May.  
- I remember calling my mom to tell her that I passed the test.  
- If I want to go into a secondary lesson, then, I remember that no one really cared/celebrated me completing all the certifications. And I didn’t really get recognized at my school for it. However, they ended up paying off because it helped get into colleges, and I even got a scholarship to UC for computer science. So, a secondary lesson would be that even if your hard work doesn’t immediately pay off, give it some time and someone will recognize what you’ve accomplished, and hopefully you’ll get rewarded for it.  
  
  
C. Clustering (Mind Map)    
Place your event in the center of a page and branch out with subtopics, emotions, people involved, and lessons learned. (You may draw this on paper or use Word’s SmartArt.)  
  
  
D. Questioning (The Five W’s and How)    
Answer the following questions about your event:    
Who? It was only me. Maybe my mom because I called her after I found out that I passed the test 
What? I took a certification test about databases, which was the last one I had left before passing all that were offered/part of our course. 
When? Junior year of high school. Beginning of May 2024 with summer break starting around mid-May 
Where? Medina, Ohio at my career tech school. 
Why? I wanted to be the first person to complete all the certifications offered for our class in one year. There was one other person who had completed all of them, but he took two years to do it, and I thought that I could do them all in one. Hence, my goal. 
How? I did a lot of studying and practice, and then there was probably a bit of luck when I took the test.  
  
  
E. Outlining Create a rough outline for your essay:    
I. Introduction – Introduce the event and the main lesson learned, maybe include a secondary one if I need more to write about. Thesis sentence about perseverance regardless of failure and how that can lead to success. 
II. Body Paragraph 1 – Explain the situation/event in more detail. My motive for why I wanted to do this. Set up the challenge. Talk about the failures and the pressure due to time and the limited number of attempts. 
III. Body Paragraph 2 – Talk about the lesson that I learned and how this has changed how I view challenges/my perseverance towards my goals. The importance of working hard to achieve self-fulfillment. 
IV. Body Paragraph 3 – How this can teach others the same lesson. Talk about how me completing the certifications inspired others in the class to also take the tests and better themselves. Steps that others can take to achieve their own goals. 
V. Conclusion – Summarize the event, lessons learned personally and for others. Reinforce perseverance and hard work to complete goals. End on a high note or something.   
  
  
F. Cubing Examine your event from six different angles:  
1. Describe it  
2. Compare it  
3. Associate it  
4. Analyze it  
5. Apply it  
6. Argue for/against it  
  
  
G. Boomerang Method Write freely about your event, then pause and reread what you wrote. Now return to the strongest idea and expand on it further.  
  
  
H. Interviewing Imagine you are interviewing someone who knows about your event (a friend, teacher, family member, or even yourself). Write at least three questions and their answers. Q1 / A1 Q2 / A2 Q3 / A3
