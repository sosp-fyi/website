### What is a not Proficient Grade (NP)? What Will Happen if You Get a Grade of NP?
A not proficient grade is where you get a grade less than a C-. It doesn't negatively affect your GPA, but it means that you will have to take the course again.

### Briefly Describe the AI Policy and Consequences of Using AI
Students are not allowed to use AI to write for them even if they revise the AI-generated response. It is allowed for minor sentence-level editing. If a student is questioned over the use of AI in their writing, they need to provide concrete evidence of the drafts for the paper. If an AI tool is used for editing, they will need to show an example of the paper before the AI edits. Consequences for using AI could be failing an assignment to failing the course and facing disciplinary action.

### A Good Scholar Understands the Content They're Citing well Enough to Converse with it even though They Didn't Read it Word for Word. How Do They Do This? (This is from Your Readings This week)
They do this through understanding what they have read and taking away the most important parts from it. For example, if someone was having a conversation about a book, they wouldn't open the book and start to read out passages to explain what happened. They would recall what they've read and then summarize it based on the most important parts. This is what a good scholar should do. They should know enough about what they are talking about that they don't need to rely on opening a book and reading from it. However, if they are trying to find a specific passage for a citation, they should also know enough about the book to know the general location for where to find what they are looking for. This is the main idea behind being conversant with a book or passage.

### What Are the Five Steps of Process Writing? What is "dangerous" about These Five Steps? (This is in Your Readings for This week)
The five steps of the writing process are: prewriting → drafting → revising → editing → publishing. The danger with seeing the five steps laid out like this is that newer, less experienced writers will think that they have to go through these steps in sequential order without going back a step or skipping around. This can lead to challenges with writing because they think that they can't move between the stages when, in reality, experienced writers move between the stages whenever necessary and aren't restricted to one stage at a time.
