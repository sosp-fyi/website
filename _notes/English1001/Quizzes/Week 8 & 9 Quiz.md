# Questions
### Why is Analyzing the Rhetorical Situation (author, Audience, Purpose, Context, genre) Important when Evaluating a Source? Provide a Brief Explanation
Analyzing the rhetorical situation is important because it helps to understand why a source was created, who it's intended for, and how it communicates its message. Also, by analyzing the rhetorical situation, the source's credibility, bias, relevance, and effectiveness can be assessed to ensure the information supports the topic/research.
### How Can Understanding Rhetorical Appeals (ethos, Pathos, logos) Help You Be a More Critical Reader or Writer? Give One Example
By understanding rhetorical appeals, you're able to analyze how authors persuade the audience, and you can evaluate the strength and credibility of their claims. For example: If an author is leaning heavily into pathos in a paper that is supposed to be focused on research, it tells you that it isn't the most reliable source because research papers should be solely objective.
### Why is it Important to Evaluate the Credibility and Reliability of a Source before Using it in Research? Provide a short Explanation
It is important to evaluate if a source is credible and reliable before using it in research because if the source isn't credible, and you use it in your paper, then the paper instantly becomes loses all credibility as well. Utilizing unreliable or non-credible sources can also weaken your arguments and, again, reduce your credibility.
