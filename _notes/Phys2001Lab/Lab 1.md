# Definitions
Independent variable (IV) - The variable that is changed by the researcher to observe what effect, if any, it has on the dependent variable.
Dependent variable (DV) - The variable that is measured by the researcher after making a change in the independent variable.
Control variable (CV) - A variable that is held constant (or controlled) because it may affect the outcome (i.e., cause a change in the DV). Note: There is often more than one CV.

## Experimental Design Template

| Research Question         | What impacts plant growth?                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dependent Variable (DV)   | Plant growth (measured by height of main plant stem)                                                                                                                                              |
| Independent variable (IV) | Amount of water received each week (Pots A: 20ml, B: 40ml, C: 60ml, D: 80ml, E: 100ml)                                                                                                            |
| Control variable (CV)     | Soil type: Miracle-Gro Potting Mix with fertilizer, Amount of light: indoor grow light (8 hrs/day), Room temperature: 70 degrees F, Pot size: 1 pint, Plant: same type, size, and health at start |
| Hypothesis                | Plant growth **is impacted by** the amount of water received each week.                                                                                                                           |
Hypothesis - Must contain IV and DV so that it is testable (may also contain prediction about outcome).
### Example Hypotheses
1. Plant growth **is impacted by** the amount of water received each week.
2. Plant growth **is affected by** the amount of water received each week.
3. Plant growth **is related to** the amount of water received each week.
4. Plant growth **is positively impacted by** the amount of water received each week so plants that receive more water will grow taller than those that receive less water.
5. Plant growth **is negatively impacted by** the amount of water received each week so plants that receive more water will not grow as tall as those that receive less water.
