# Simulations vs. Digital Twins
### Simulations
- Follow equations/rules of model precise
#### Real-world Systems
- Deviate from the pure equation-based models
- Individual pieces of pendulum differ from each other
- Wind patterns on roads are different from the ones in wind-tunnels
- Temperatures for circuits are different from the ones assumed in simulations

Digital Twins in aerospace industry: [Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7yjQKRPcY8E)

### Digital Twins Include
- Systems' equations
- Complex with multiple components
- A virtual environment for the system
	- Environmental factors of the system
	- Temp, wind velocities, effects of other systems
- Data flow from outside the simulation

# Digital Twins History
- The name “digital twin” was introduced by NASA's John Vickers in 2010
- The same idea under the name “mirror worlds” was introduced by David Gelernter in 1991
- The idea was first applied to manufacturing by Dr. Michael Grieves in 2002
- This same core idea has been used (without any name) by NASA to study the behaviors of spacecrafts since the 1960s.

# Digital Twins of Engineering Systems
Types of projects that benefit from digital twins:
- Physically large projects: bridges, tunnels, skyscraper buildings
- Complex systems: jet engines, turbines, automobiles, aircraft
	- “What if…” questions can be simulated in digital twins for answers
- Manufacturing processes and factories
