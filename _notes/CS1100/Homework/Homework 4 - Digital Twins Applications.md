# List of Applications for Digital Twins from [Duke University](https://comphealth.duke.edu/digital-twins-in-healthcare-revolutionizing-patient-care-at-duke/)
- Proactive monitoring
- Surgical planning
- Long-term outcome prediction
- Personalized treatment
- Simulating surgeries
- Insights into patient's anatomy and physiology on an individual level
## Role These Applications Can Play
1. Proactive monitoring of a patient can help spot issues in their health before they become a danger to the patient. This can help reduce the amount of time that a patient might have to stay in the hospital.
2. Surgical planning can help a surgeon decide the best course of action for a particular surgery based on data specific to the patient. This can reduce the amount of complication during or after the surgery, giving the best chance of success. 
3. Long-term outcome prediction can simulate the progression of a disease or even a particular remedy to a solution. This would allow doctors to choose the best course of action based on the data. It could also be integrated into the proactive monitoring to catch issues before they happen.
4. Personalized treatment using digital twins can use a patient's personalized data to tailor the best treatment plan for that specific person. This can reduce trial and error approaches and improve recovery times.
5. Simulating surgeries can help a surgeon understand this patient better and give a greater chance of success during the surgery. By doing this, there would also be less chance of complications and reduce potential issues for the patient down the line.
# Validating a Digital Twin
Validating a digital twin, such as one used for surgical planning, would mean insuring the model accurately represents the patient's anatomy and physiological conditions. This process would involve data accuracy, model validation, and clinical trials. Data accuracy would check that all data being using by the digital twin is up-to-date, accurate, and precise. Model validation would compare the digital twin against clinical outcomes to assess its accuracy and consequently its usefulness. Clinical trials would include using the digital twin in studies where the digital twins outcomes are compared to real-world results to confirm reliability. 

If I were tasked with validating this type of digital twin, I would use the above steps to make sure that the digital twin works as intended. I would also add on a check for ease of use by doctors and staff that would be using the system.
# Challenges for Designing a Digital Twin
## Challenges for Designing a Digital Twin
- Managing and utilizing the vast amounts of data
- Data privacy and security
- Integrating wearable technology for proactive monitoring
	- Collaboration between healthcare providers and the technological systems
	- Significant investments into infrastructure and training
## Suggested Solutions
- Standardization: If companies developed standards that they would all use, the integration process would be much easier, albeit still difficult.
- Collaboration between companies: If there was a singular place where all the companies could come together to share information, it would do a lot to streamline the development process.
- Data analytics: Use AI models to handle data verification and analysis of large datasets instead of humans. Have checks for confusing data points or issues in the data. This is already being developed, but it is still worth mentioning.
## Subfields of Computer Science for Accomplishing These Tasks
- AI and machine learning: For data analysis, pattern recognition, and predictive modeling.
- Cybersecurity: To protect patients data and securely handle communication between devices and systems. 
- Data science: For managing large datasets and ensuring data quality and usability.
- Human-computer interaction: Used to design systems that are intuitive for users and interact with digital twin systems efficiently.
