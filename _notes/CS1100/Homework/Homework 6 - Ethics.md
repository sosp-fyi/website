1. The ethical challenge of biases from training data arises when AI systems learn and replicate existing prejudices present in their datasets. For example, facial recognition systems often perform worse on darker skin tones, and hiring algorithms may favor male candidates due to biased historical data. Allowing such bias to persist violates the ACM Code of Ethics principle of _avoiding harm to others_ and _treating all people fairly_.
2. 
	1. The ethical challenge for privacy of data in AI systems comes from the systems to collecting, storing, and using individuals' personal information without proper consent or protection.
	2. For example, an AI healthcare system that stores patient data insecurely risks exposing sensitive medical records if a cybersecurity breach occurs.
	3. By failing to protect privacy can lead to identity theft, discrimination, and a loss of trust from the public in technology.
	4. The ACM Code of Conduct emphasizes respecting privacy and maintaining confidentiality, requiring professionals to keep personal data safe and only use it for legitimate purposes.
