# Introduction

The objectives for experiment 6 were to measure the light absorbance of a solution, prepare a plot of absorbance vs. concentration (Beer's Law plot), and measure the absorbance of a sports drink and use the previously determined plot to determine concentration. The light absorbance of the solution was determined by placing the solution in a cuvette and inserting that into the spectrometer, which then told showed the absorbance. The Beer's Law plot was created by inserting all the concentration and absorbance data into Excel and then creating a line plot to graph the data. The concentration of the sports drink was determined by taking the slope from the graph and dividing the sports drink absorbance by the slope. 

The lecture concepts used in this lab were spectroscopy, dilutions, and wavelengths.

The lab techniques were using a volumetric pipet, using a spectrometer, and graphing absorbance using the LabQuest system.

# **1.** What Are the Advantages of Selecting the Wavelength of Maximum Absorption for Determining Absorbance Values for Preparation of a Beer’s Law Plot?

Selecting the wavelength of maximum absorption increases the sensitivity, precision, and linearity in a Beer's Law plot. This leads to the most accurate and reproducible concentration determinations.
