# Reversible & Irreversible Processes
- A reversible process is one in which every point along some path is an equilibrium state
	- & for which the system can be returned to its initial state along the same path
- An irreversible process does not meet these requirements
	- all natural processes are known to be irreversible
- A real process that is a good approximation of a reversible one will occur very slowly. A general characteristic of a reversible process is that there are no dissipative effects that convert mechanical energy to internal energy present
	- (No friction or turbulence, for example)

# Carnot Cycle
Successive isothermal, adiabatic processes
1. The working substance (gas) absorbs energy by heat from a high temperature energy reservoir ($Q_h$). Isothermal
2. Work is done by the engine (adiabatic)
3. Energy is expelled as heat to a lower temperature reservoir ($Q_c$). Isothermal
4. Work is done to the engine (adiabatic)

## Carnot's Theorem
- No real heat engine operating between two energy reservoirs can be more efficient than a Carnot engine operating between the same two reservoirs
- Reverse cycle: Carnot heat pump would have the maximum possible COPs for a given combination of hot and cold reservoirs
(Real cycles are not truly reversible, due to friction, energy losses through conduction, etc.)

# Second Law of Thermodynamics
- Entropy of the universe increases → Entropy formulation
- Heat doesn’t flow from cold to hot spontaneously → Clausius statement
- No engine more efficient than Carnot → Carnot principle
- Cannot convert all heat into work in a cycle → Kelvin–Planck statement
