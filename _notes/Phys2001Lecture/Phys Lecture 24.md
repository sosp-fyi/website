# Torque
Force is related to translational acceleration, by Newton's 2nd law
- What role does force play in angular acceleration for an extended object that can rotate freely about a fixed axis?

Torque varies with angle between radius and force
$\alpha$ depends on r

magnitude for torque is:
$$
\tau = rFsin\phi
$$
SI unit: N · m
Dimension is the same as for work/energy
- Not physically the same

or  
$d = rsin\phi$  
$\tau=dF$
or  
$\tau=rF⟂$

Torque is a vector x vector product but of a different type than the scalar product (e.g., work)

## Torque is a Vector (1D for now)
Rotation in a plane
- Sign +/-, is same as for angular displacement
	- + counterclockwise
	- - clockwise
