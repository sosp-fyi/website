# Non-isolated System
sigma F = dp/dt momentum is changed under the influence of force over a period of time
the change in momentum is the integral of net force vs. time
$$
p_f - p_i = \int_{t_i}^{t_f}\Sigma F dt
$$
impulse-momentum theorem
$\Delta p = I$
note: p, have same dimension, different SI units
momentum: mv → kg m/s
impulse: Ft → N s

# Collision
- an event during which
	- 2 particles come close to each other and interact by means of forces
	- time interval where velocity change is "short"
	- external forces can be ignored

Model: isolated system (momentum)
- Momentum is always conserved
- 2 types of collisions
	- Elastic: kinetic energy is conserved
	- Inelastic: kinetic energy is not conserved
		- Perfectly inelastic: objects are stuck together, either before or after.
