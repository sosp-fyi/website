# Chapter 5 - The Laws of Motion
## Force
A force is a 
- push or pull
- an interaction between two objects or between an object and its environment
- vector quantity, with magnitude and direction

The SI unit of force is a newton (N). 
1 N = 1 kg m/s $^2$
### Gravitational Force (Non-Contact)
The attractive force that the Earth exerts on objects close to its surface
- Symbol: F $_g$ or W
- Magnitude = F $_g$ = mg (mass * 9.8 m/s $^2$)
- Direction = downwards (towards the center of the Earth)
### Normal Force
The force a surface is exerting on an object in contact with it in order to keep the object from going through it
- Symbol: F $_N$ or N
- Magnitude = depends on the situation
- Direction = perpendicular to the surface
### Tension Force
The force a rope or string exerts on an object
- Symbol: F $_T$ or T
- Magnitude = depends on the situation
- Direction = through the string, opposite point of contact = ropes/strings are used to pull objects

## Inertia
The tendency of an object to resist any attempt to change its velocity
## Mass
the property of an object that specifies how much resistance an object exhibits to changes in its velocity.
## Weight
The weight of an object is equal to the magnitude of the gravitational force exerted on the object and varies with location.
# Newton's 2nd Law
$\overrightarrow{a}$ = $\frac{\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{net}}}{m}$ Often rearranged and used as $\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{net}} = m\overrightarrow{a}$ 

$\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{net}}$ not F
$\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{net}}$ = sum of all forces acting on object
There is no such thing as "force equals ma"
# Newton's 3rd Law
Every action (force) has an equal and opposite reaction
When two objects A and B interact, $\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{A -> B}} = -\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{B -> A}}$
Better expressed as:
- | $\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{A -> B}}$ | = | $\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{B -> A}}$ |, or $\overrightarrow{F}_{\text{A -> B}} = \overrightarrow{F}_{\text{B -> A}}$
- Directions: opposite
### Very Important
1. Newton's third law (force) pairs act on different objects
2. Newton's third law (force) pairs, must be expressible in this form: Force A exerts on B and Force B exerts on A
If 1 and 2 are not true for two forces, they are not Newton's third law force pairs.
