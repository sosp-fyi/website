# The Vector Product and Torque
The vector (or cross) product of $\overrightarrow A$, $\overrightarrow B$ "A cross B"
$$
\overrightarrow C = \overrightarrow A \times \overrightarrow B
$$

- vector
	- magnitude c = AB sin $\theta$ ( $\theta$ = angle between A and B)
- Direction: perpendicular to the plane formed by $\overrightarrow A$, $\overrightarrow B$
right-hand rule

A vector w magnitude is C = AB sin $\theta$

Torque is a vector product
$$
\overrightarrow \tau = \overrightarrow r \times \overrightarrow F
$$
magnitude $\tau = rFsin \theta$
Direction: right-hand rule
Notation for x-y plane "in the page" + "out of page" - "into the page"

# Angular Momentum
SI unit: (kg * m $^2$)/s
$\overrightarrow L = \overrightarrow r \times \overrightarrow p$
## Systems of Particles
The total angular momentum of a system = vector sum of the angular momenta of particles
$$
\overrightarrow l_{tot} = \sum_i \overrightarrow L_i
$$
Torque on system
$$
\frac{d \overrightarrow L_{tot}}{dt} = \sum_i \overrightarrow \tau_i
$$
Any torques associated with internal forces sum to zero
$$
\frac{d \overrightarrow L_{tot}}{dt} = \sum_i \overrightarrow \tau_i
$$

For particle $\overrightarrow L = I \overrightarrow \omega$
Angular velocity: direction of rotation axis right-hand rule
