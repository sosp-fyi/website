# Linear Momentum
if m1 and m2 are parts of the same system, the total force on the system is the vector sum of all forces on all parts of the system.
According to Newton's 3rd law, the sum of the force pair, $F_{21} + F_{12}$ is always zero.

$$
\overrightarrow{F} = ma = \frac{d(m\overrightarrow{v})}{dt}
$$
$$
\overrightarrow{p} = m\overrightarrow{v}
$$
SI unit kg * m/s
momentum is a vector quantity
$$
p_x = mv_x p_y = mv_yp_z = mv_z
$$
The total momentum of a system is the sum of the momenta of its parts

The total momentum is constant in time, i.e., conserved.

# Isolated Systems
For a system, total linear momentum is the vector sum over all particles in the system
$$
\overrightarrow{p_{tot}} = \Sigma
$$
In an isolated system with >1 particles
- No external forces
- Forces between particles (internal): Newton's 3rd law holds at every instant
Total linear momentum is constant in time (conserved).
