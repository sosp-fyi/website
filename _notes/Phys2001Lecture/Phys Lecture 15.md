# Conservative Forces
- gravity
- spring force
- other fundamental forces (electromagnetic)

Characteristics
- force on a given object may depend on its position (but not on anything else: other forces, velocity)
- work done by force on object moving between any 2 points is independent of path
- work done by force on object moving on any closed path is zero
# Nonconservative Forces
- A nonconservative force does not satisfy the conditions of conservative forces
	- The work done on a particle moving between any two points depends on the path taken by the particle
	- the work done on a particle moving through any closed path is non-zero
- Examples
	- normal force
	- tension
	- friction
- Potential energy is not a meaningful concept
	- nonconservative forces can cause a change in the mechanical energy of the system

Energy is conserved in the universe
- energy cannot be created nor destroyed
- energy in a system can only be changed by transfer of energy in or out through the system boundary
- there are many types of energy
	- work, kinetic
	- waves
	- heat (internal energy)
	- matter (E=mc $^2$)
	- electromagnetic
	- gravitational

# Isolated System
In an isolated system, no energy leaves or enters the system

if there are no nonconservative forces doing work in an isolated system, then 
- total mechanical energy
$$
E_{\text{mech}} = K + U
$$
is constant, i.e., mechanical energy is conserved
$$
\Delta E_{\text{mech}} = \Delta K + \Delta U = 0
$$

# Situations Involving Kinetic Friction
Force of kinetic friction is nonconservative
- an object with nonzero speed on a surface with friction slows down reduces K (even with no other forces doing work)
- What happens to the energy?
	- Adds to internal energy $\Delta E_{\text{int}}$ (of the object and surface)

If we consider the object and surface to be of the same system
- K that is converted to $E_{\text{int}}$ to stays inside the system
- Including $E_{\text{int}}$ as system energy allows the expansion of the energy conservation principle

# Changes in Mechanical Energy for Nonconservative Forces
Isolated system, internal friction
- work by friction reduces kinetic energy what happens to it
- friction increases internal energy (thermal); energy is conserved if internal energy is included
