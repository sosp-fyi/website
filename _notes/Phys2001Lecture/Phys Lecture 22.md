For non-uniform circular motion compared to uniform (same v, r at an instant), which is true of acceleration?
- Direction is not the same, radial component is always the same.

For uniform circular motion, acceleration has
- constant magnitude
- direction: toward the center (centripetal) perpendicular to velocity

For non-uniform circular motion, acceleration has
- changing speed
	- tangential component, parallel to velocity
	- centripetal (radial) component.
$$
a_r = -a_c = -\frac{v^2}{r}
$$
At any position there is a unique circle that fits the path
- the speed gives the centripetal (radial) component of acceleration
- the rate of change gives the tangential component.
