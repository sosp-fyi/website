# Particle under Constant Acceleration
if a(t) is constant
and a(t) is the first derivative of v(t)
$$
a_x = lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{\Delta v_x}{\Delta t} = \frac{d v_x}{dt}
$$
$$
\frac{v_f - v_i}{t_f - t_i}
$$
derive v(t)
$$
a_x t_f = v_f - v(0)
$$
$$
v_f = at + v_0
$$
$a_x$ is constant

derive x(t)
$$
x_f -x_i = \int_{t_i}^{t_f}v
$$
$$
x_f = x_i + v_{xi}t + \frac{1}{2}a_x t^2
$$
### Average Velocity
$$
v_{xavg} = \frac{v_{xf} + v_{xi}}{2}
$$
### Displacement and Final X, given Velocities at the Endpoints
$$
\Delta x = v_{x,avg} \frac{\Delta t}{t}
$$
$$
= (\frac{v_{xi}+v_{xf}}{2})t
$$
$$
x_f = x_i + (\frac{v_{xi}+v_{xf}}{2})t
$$
$$
x_f = x_i + \frac{1}{2}(v_{xi} + v_{xf})t
$$
### Equation for the Final Velocity, given Initial Velocity Displacement and Acceleration
$$
v_{xf} = v_{xi} + a_x t
$$
$$
\Delta x = \frac{1}{2} (v_{xi}+v{xf})t
$$
$$
t = \frac{1}{2}\frac{v_{xi}+v_{xf}}{\Delta x}
$$
$$
2a_(x_f - x_i) = v_{xf}^2 - v_{xi}^2
$$
$$
v_{xf}^2 = v_{xi}^2 + 2a_x(x_f - x_i)
$$
