In pure rolling motion, an object rolls without slipping.
- Relationship between rotation and distance traveled is $s=R\theta$. Arc length and distance traveled.
- Relationship between translational speed and angular speed is $v=R𝜔$ or $𝜔=\frac{v}{R}$ 

The point of contact between the rolling object and the ground has 0 instantaneous velocity (when there is no slipping) because at that moment there is no velocity on the bottom point.

Pure rotation about fixed point angular speed = $\omega$
rotational KE = $\frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$

Parallel axis theorem:
$$
I = I_{CM}+MD^2
$$

A rotating object has more kinetic energy than a non-rotating object and the amount depends on the shape of the object.
