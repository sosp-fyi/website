# Molecular Model of an Ideal Gas
State variables P, V, T, E $_\text{int}$; transfer variables Q, W
- Are macroscopic:
	- model of matter as a continuous medium, laws from observation
But microscopically
- Gas is composed of molecules
	- Newton's laws of motion, applied statistically (averages, spreads)
	- the microscopic model must quantitatively explain state variables, macroscopic model & observation
Ideal gas molecular model:
- Large (huge!) number of identical molecules
- random movement: any direction
- short-range elastic collisions only (think Ballard balls)

Pressure
- Pressure is from (many) gas molecules bouncing elastically off the container wall
- - Momentum conservation: molecule ↔ wall
- m $\Delta v$ = F $\Delta t$ (momentum-impulse)
- pressure = F/A
Depends on density of molecules, average speed
$$
P = \frac{2}{3} \frac{N}{V} (\frac{1}{2} m_o v^2)
$$
relationship between the gas temperature and the average transnational kinetic energy
$$
K = \frac{3}{2}k_B T
$$
# Molar Specific Heat of a (monatomic) Ideal Gas
Recall specific heat: $Q=mc\Delta T$
Molar specific heat C is defined = heat to raise T of 1 mole of atoms/molecules by 1 C
$Q = nC\Delta T$
$C = \frac{Q}{n\Delta T}$
- For gasses, a bit complicated consider 1st law
$$
\Delta E_\text{int} = \frac{3}{2} nR\Delta T
$$
$$
C = \frac{3}{2}R-\frac{W}{n\Delta T}
$$

Not a constant

C for different paths
isovolumetric: $\frac{3R}{2} = C_v$
isobaric: $\frac{5R}{2} = C_p$

Derived quantities of interest
$$
C_P - C_V = R
$$
Ratio
$$
\gamma = \frac{C_P}{C_V} = \frac{5}{3}
$$
