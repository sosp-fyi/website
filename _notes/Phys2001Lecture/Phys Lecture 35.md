# Heat & Internal Energy
Internal energy: all the energy that is associated with microscopic components (atoms/molecules) of a system when viewed from a reference frame at rest with respect to its center of mass

Heat: a process of energy transfer across a system boundary due to a temperature difference between the system and its surroundings; the amount of energy Q transferred by this process

Unit of heat (Q): calorie
- amount of energy transfer necessary to raise the temp of 1 g of water from 14.5 C to 15.5 C
- 1 cal = 4.186 J "the mechanical equivalent of heat"
- dietary calories: 1 Cal = 1 kcal = 10 $^3$ cal

# Specific Heat & Calorimetry
Adding internal energy may result in
- Temperature rise
- phase transition (solid ↔ liquid ↔ gas)

If adding energy Q to a system produces temperature change $\Delta T$, then
$$
Q = C \Delta T
$$
C = heat capacity of system = amount of energy needed to raise T by 1 C

Specific heat, c = heat capacity per unit mass
$$
c = C/m 
$$
$$
=> Q = C \Delta T = mc \Delta T
$$
Specific heat depends on material
- Varies with temperature, in general
- Is nearly constant for some materials

# Calorimetry
Measuring an unknown specific heat
- Isolated system; no energy exchange with outside
while $Q_w = -Q_x$ or the heat is transferred to the water from the substance.
$$
c_x = \frac{m_wc_w(T_f-T_w)}{m_x(T_x-T_f)}
$$
# Latent Heat
Phases of matter: liquid, solid, gas
phase change: liquid ↔ gas; solid ↔ liquid; solid ↔ gas
- occurs at specific temperatures (melting point, boiling point)
- heat is transferred, but temperature is unchanged
- internal energy changes
- amount of heat transfer Q 
$Q = L\Delta m$ 
L = latent heat: specific to material, type of phase change

# Work in Thermodynamic Processes (gases)
State variables: in internal thermal equilibrium
P, V, T, E $_{\text{int}}$
Transfer variables: associated with change of state
work (W), heat (Q)

Work:
- Gas has pressure → exerts force on piston:
$F = PA$
- Newton's 3$^{\text{rd}}$: piston exerts force on gas
- work by piston for displacement dy
$$
dW = Fdy = PAdy = -P dV
$$
dv = volume changes of gas
- Work by gas is -dW
