# Temperature & Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics
What is temperature (T)? (what is heat)?
- Is/are either/both
	- quantifiable
	- reproducible
	- related to the other

Heat is a measure of internal energy
Temperature
- For "ideal gas", a measure of average kinetic energy
- More generally defined by the Zeroth law of thermodynamics
## Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics
2 objects are 
- in thermal contact with each other if
	- energy in the form of heat or electromagnetic radiation can be exchanged due to the temperature difference between them
- in thermal equilibrium with each other if
	- they would not exchange energy (heat or EM radiation) if placed in thermal contact
- at the same temp if 
	- they are in thermal equilibrium with each other
Zeroth law of thermodynamics
If objects A and B are separately in thermal equilibrium with a third object c then a and b are in equilibrium with each other.

## Thermal Expansion of Solids and Liquids
Many materials increase in size with increase in temperature.

Length (solids):
$$
\Delta L = \alpha L_i \Delta T
$$
$\alpha$ coefficient of linear expansion = 1/(temp)
$$
L_f - L_i = \alpha L_i(T_f - T_i)
$$
Volume expansion (liquids):
$$
\Delta V = \beta V_i \Delta T
$$
$\beta$ coefficient of volume expansion 
$\beta = 3\alpha$
