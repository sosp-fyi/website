# Uniform Circular Motion
The time it takes to travel around a full circle is called the period, T

speed = velocity = distance / time
$= \frac{2\pi r}{T}$

Velocity is tangent to the circle

Acceleration
path length / time
$$
a = \frac{2 \pi v}{T}
$$
$$
a = \frac{v^2}{r}
$$
acceleration points in the opposite direction of the position vector.


Rate of change of the angular velocity
$\omega = \frac{v}{r}$ or $\frac{2\pi}{T}$
angles are dimensionless

Summary
- Motion repeats (is cyclic)
	- period t=time for one complete revolution
	- v = speed = delta L / delta t tangential
	- a = acceleration = v^2/r radial (inward)
- polar coordinate system (r = constant)
	- theta = angular position L/r unit: radian (dimensionless)

magnitude of the net force acting on a mass M in uniform circular motion with speed v and radius r is:
$M v^2 / r$
